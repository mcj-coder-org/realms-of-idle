# Dwarves

> **See [Base Traits](base.md)** for shared racial characteristics, lore, and cultural notes.

## Variants

Dwarves have adapted to various environments and specializations, creating distinct regional variants while maintaining their core identity as master crafters and enduring warriors.

### Mountain Dwarf

Traditional dwarves of the high peaks, representing classical dwarven culture at its most concentrated. Masters of weapons and armor, with the strongest martial traditions and most conservative values. They view themselves as the keepers of true dwarven heritage.

**Key Features:** +1 STR, superior weapons and armor, martial excellence

**[Learn More →](mountain.md)**

### Hill Dwarf

Surface-adapted dwarves who bridge the gap between underground holds and surface communities. They maintain dwarven crafting traditions while developing diplomatic skills and specializing in brewing and farming. Most personable of all dwarven variants.

**Key Features:** +1 CHA, legendary brewing, surface integration, diplomatic skills

**[Learn More →](hill.md)**

### Deep Dwarf

Mysterious dwarves of the deepest depths, delving miles below where other dwarves stop. Masters of gems and rare metals found only under extreme pressure, with enhanced darkvision and isolated culture. They're the most alien of dwarven variants, shaped by eternal darkness and crushing depths.

**Key Features:** Enhanced darkvision, rare gems and metals, extreme depth specialization

**[Learn More →](deep.md)**

### Forge Dwarf

Volcanic dwarves who harness natural heat to achieve smithing temperatures impossible elsewhere. Master smiths even by dwarven standards, working exotic alloys and rare metals. Heat-adapted physiology allows them to work in conditions that would kill unprotected smiths.

**Key Features:** +25% heat resistance, exotic metals, adamantine/mithril mastery

**[Learn More →](forge.md)**

## Choosing a Variant

### Choose Mountain Dwarf if

- You want to play a traditional warrior dwarf with martial excellence
- You prefer strength-based combat and front-line fighting
- You appreciate conservative values and ancient traditions
- You want to create legendary weapons and armor
- You're drawn to the classic "dwarven warrior" archetype

### Choose Hill Dwarf if

- You want enhanced social capabilities (rare for dwarves)
- You enjoy bridging cultures and serving as party diplomat
- You're interested in brewing, farming, or civilian crafts
- You prefer surface adventures over deep dungeon delving
- You want to play a more approachable, friendly dwarf character

### Choose Deep Dwarf if

- You love underground exploration and dungeon delving
- You want to work with rare, exotic materials
- You enjoy playing isolated, mysterious characters
- You value patience and perfectionism in crafting
- You're drawn to the deepest, darkest parts of the world
- Superior darkvision matters for your playstyle

### Choose Forge Dwarf if

- You want to be the ultimate smith, creating masterwork items
- Heat resistance matters for your campaign or character concept
- You enjoy working with exotic metals like adamantine or mithril
- You appreciate pride backed by genuine exceptional skill
- You want to push the boundaries of what's metallurgically possible
- You're willing to deal with volcanic environments

## Available Classes

Dwarves have **unrestricted access to all classes** with strong affinity to crafting and martial classes:

**All Classes Available**: Every class is accessible to dwarves regardless of variant

- Dwarves excel at: Blacksmith, Carpenter, Jeweler, Warrior, Guard (crafting/martial)
- Dwarves can access: All magic classes, rogues, traders, and other professions

**Strength by Class Type**:

- **Crafting Classes**: Mountain Home traditions make dwarves exceptional crafters
- **Martial Classes**: Combat heritage and endurance provide warrior advantages
- **Magic Classes**: Accessible but less traditional; dwarves can master any magic path
- **Gathering Classes**: Mining heritage makes Miner and related paths natural fits

**Advantage**: Dwarven durability and tradition pair well with skilled trades, but you can pursue any class without limitation. Your centuries-long lifespan allows mastery in multiple fields.
