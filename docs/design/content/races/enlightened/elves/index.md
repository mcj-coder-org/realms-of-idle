# Elves

> **See [Base Traits](base/index.md)** for shared racial characteristics, lore, and cultural notes.

## Variants

Elves have adapted to various environments across the world, each variant developing distinct magical affinities, cultural practices, and specializations while maintaining their core identity as long-lived, magically-gifted beings.

### High Elf

Elves of the ancient cities, representing elven culture at its most refined and magically advanced. Masters of arcane magic and accumulated knowledge, with the longest-lived and most scholarly traditions. They view themselves as the exemplars of elven civilization.

**Key Features:** Magic/Knowledge mastery, Arcane/Abjuration magic, scholarly excellence

**[Learn More →](high/index.md)**

### Wood Elf

Forest-dwelling elves who blend seamlessly with the natural world. Masters of stealth and survival, with deep connections to nature and exceptional tracking abilities. They're the most practical and approachable of all elven variants, though still distinctly elven in outlook.

**Key Features:** Nature magic, forest mastery, enhanced stealth, tracking excellence

**[Learn More →](wood/index.md)**

### Sea Elf

Coastal and oceanic elves who adapted to life on and beneath the waves. Master sailors and underwater explorers, with unique abilities to breathe water and swim with supernatural grace. They connect coastal settlements and maintain mysterious underwater cities.

**Key Features:** Water breathing, enhanced swimming, Water/Conjuration magic, maritime mastery

**[Learn More →](sea/index.md)**

### Dusk Elf

Underground-dwelling elves who embraced shadow magic and nocturnal existence. The most mysterious and socially complicated elven variant, with perfect darkvision and controversial necromantic practices. They maintain their own civilization separate from mainstream elven society.

**Key Features:** Perfect darkvision, Shadow/Necromancy magic, underground adaptation

**[Learn More →](dusk/index.md)**

## Choosing a Variant

### Choose High Elf if

- You want to play the quintessential elven mage with maximum magical potential
- You value knowledge, scholarship, and accumulated wisdom
- You enjoy playing refined, cultured characters from ancient civilizations
- You prefer arcane and protective magic over nature or shadow
- You're drawn to courtly intrigue and centuries-old politics
- You want enhanced spellcasting capabilities and magical research

### Choose Wood Elf if

- You prefer nature over cities and forests over libraries
- You enjoy stealth, tracking, and survival skills
- You want to play a more grounded, practical elf character
- You're drawn to ranger or druid concepts
- You value harmony with nature and woodland creatures
- You want to be more approachable than typical high elves
- Forest environments are central to your character concept

### Choose Sea Elf if

- Your adventures center on coastal, oceanic, or island settings
- You want aquatic versatility with water breathing and enhanced swimming
- You enjoy exploration, trade, and connecting distant settlements
- You're drawn to the mystery of underwater civilizations
- You want Water or Conjuration magic specialization
- You prefer adaptability and fluid problem-solving
- Nautical campaigns appeal to you

### Choose Dusk Elf if

- You're interested in shadow magic and necromancy
- Perfect darkvision in absolute darkness matters for your playstyle
- You enjoy playing misunderstood or socially complicated characters
- Underground environments appeal to you
- You want to explore controversial magical practices
- You prefer independence from mainstream society
- You're drawn to the tension between elven heritage and dark magic

## Available Classes

Elves have **unrestricted access to all classes** with magical affinity bonuses to classes aligned with their variant:

**All Classes Available**: Every class is accessible to elves regardless of variant

- Elves excel at: Mage, Illusionist, Summoner, Shaman (magic-focused classes)
- Elves can access: All fighters, crafters, gatherers, traders, rogues, consolidation classes

**Magic Affinity by Variant**:

- **High Elf**: Bonus to Arcane/Abjuration magic (Mage paths, Enchanter)
- **Wood Elf**: Bonus to Nature/Illusion magic (Shaman, Druid-adjacent roles)
- **Sea Elf**: Bonus to Water/Conjuration magic (Summoner, maritime classes)
- **Dusk Elf**: Bonus to Shadow/Necromancy magic (Necromancer, dark magic users)

**Advantage**: Elves combine magical heritage with unrestricted class access. While you'll be strongest with magic-aligned classes, you can pursue any class without limitation.
